Working at Height

This talk covers what you need to think about and do, before and during working at height

All work at height must be properly planned. Around half of all fatalities on construction sites are as a result of falls from height

Hazards

  • The incorrect selection or use of access equipment for the task is one of the biggest causes of falls from height. Never use the wrong piece of equipment just because that is all you have available.
  • Adverse weather conditions must be anticipated and suitable precautions taken - beware of wet, windy or icy weather.
  • Too much material on a working a working platfrom can make access difficult or make the platfrom unstable.
  • Don't allow rubbish to accumulate. Use a chute or lower materials properly.

Before working at height

  • All work at height must be avoided where possible. If work at height cannot be avoided then a collective system to prevent falling (such as scaffolding, a MEWP or a mobile tower) should be used.
  • All work at height must be carried out in accordance with the safe system of work provided.
  • The consequences of any fall should be minimised by using an appropriate soft-landing system, net or - as a last resort - a safety harness
  • The correct work equipment that is suitable for the task must be provided.
  • All equipment provided must be inspected and maintained.
  • Ensure you receive the correct information, instruction and training on the safe use of access equipment.
  • Ensure you have the right skills, knowledge, training and experience for the job.
  • Ladders and stepladders should only be used for light work of a short duration and where there is a low risk of falling.
  • Fall protection (such as edge protection systems, barriers and perimeter scaffolds) should be installed to prevent the risk of materials or persons falling.
  • Access ladders must extend at least 1M above the the stepping off point, be secured, and rest at the correct angle (one unit out to four units up or 75 degrees)

Safe working on roofs

  • Fragile surfaces (such as cement roof sheets and skylights) must be identified with signs. Measures should be taken to stop you falling through a fragile surface
  • A safe system of work must be devised and implemented where the roof would otherwise be liable to collapse under a person's weight. A temporary playform is normally used.
  • If edge protection or a soft landing system cannot be used, it may be necessary to use a saftey harness and lanyard. A secure, designated anchorage point and training will be neccessary.
  • Openings must have a securely fixed cover or guard; if removed for any reason, additional protection must be installed and the cover or guard replaced as soon as is practicable.
  • Safe means of access must be provided (such as temporary staircases or fixed ladder with a gate).
  • Bitumen boilers require a fire-resistant drip tray and appropriate fire extinguisher
